Decisions I made for you
Layout: source leads
Source and state side-by-side inside the 480px band. Source is the subject; state is the read-out. Scrubber spans the bottom; the counter lives in the head.
480px holds — barely
It fits with room to breathe. I do not recommend a taller frame; the discipline keeps it reading as a band in the page, not an app.
Changed vs unchanged
What moved this step gets sienna ink + a ◆ marker. Everything else dims to ink-4 so the eye lands on the change.
Large values = chips
A type + shape chip (DataFrame · 1000×12) you click to expand. Opaque types (connections) never get repr'd.
Long traces fold
Jump-to-next-change, change markers on the track, and folded loop iterations keep a 612-step trace navigable.
Motion: calm transitions
The line highlight slides; changed values ease in color. No flashing — comprehension, not spectacle.

Type + shape chip
Every large value reads as type · shape first. The shape is enough to recognize what it is without serializing it.
Expand on demand
A dict shows its keys; a DataFrame shows its schema (never 1000 rows); a list shows first-N + a count. The panel never floods.
Opaque = dashed, never repr'd
Connections, sockets, ORM proxies get a dashed ‹Connection› tag — the skip-list protects capture from a __repr__ that hits the network.
§4

Step explosion — 612 steps, still navigable

A loop blows the step count up. The scrubber marks every change; jump to next change skips the in-between; repeated iterations fold. Hit “next change” →.

Jump to next change
The primary verb for long traces. Steps where nothing in scope moved are skipped — you land only where state actually changed.
Change markers
Sienna ticks on the track are a minimap of where things happened — so 612 steps read as a handful of moments, not a smear.
Folded iterations
Repeated loop passes collapse into a hatched band — expand only the iteration you care about. The counter still tells the honest total.
