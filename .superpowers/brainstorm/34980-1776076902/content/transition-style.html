Zoom/Explode Transition Style

When you click a node to enter it, how does the camera move and how does the content appear?

A

Cinematic Fly-Through

Camera accelerates toward the node, passes through the "surface", and decelerates into the interior space. Like entering a wormhole. The surrounding universe fades and the inner contents materialize.

B

Smooth Expand-in-Place

The node grows and splits open, revealing its children. Other nodes fade out and drift away. Like a cell dividing under a microscope. The camera stays mostly still.

C

Both: Quick expand + fly-through for deep zoom

Level 1→2 (project → folders): smooth expand. Level 2→3 (folder → files): fly-through. Level 3→4 (file → functions): expand again. Alternating styles give each level a distinct feel.

Universe → Galaxy: expand-in-place (quick)
Galaxy → Star System: cinematic fly-through (dramatic)
Star System → Planet: expand-in-place (quick)
Best of both. Variety prevents animation fatigue. Each level feels different.
MY RECOMMENDATION
C — Alternating styles. A full fly-through on every click gets repetitive. Alternating between expand and fly-through gives each zoom level its own personality and prevents animation fatigue. The dramatic fly-through is reserved for the most important transition (entering a folder/module), while the quick expand handles the lighter transitions.
